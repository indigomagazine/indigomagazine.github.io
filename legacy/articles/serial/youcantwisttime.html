You Can Twist Time

Written by Jiya Gupta

The metallic smell of blood wafted through the breeze. The green grass was now dark red. I stood on top of the bodies. The knife clutched in my hand shook. My whole body shook from excitement. There was just such an exhilarating sensation that shot through my body when I realized that I was in control of someone’s life.

The adrenaline that made my heart thunder in my ears when they fought back against me. The stinging pain of the scratches they left on my body filled me with a sense of pride. The satisfaction that coursed through my veins after the light in their eyes slowly faded away. The– oh?

...You think I’m crazy?

Maybe… maybe you're right.

But you don’t know what I had to go through. It’s not like I wanted to do this. We used to be recognized together, as a group. Me, History, and Religion. People used to call us the perfect trio: harmoniously complimenting each other, like the flavors of your favorite homecooked dish.

But then, people started paying more attention to me. I became mainstream. And something– something changed. It first started with irritation. Every single little thing they did set me off. I exploded with anger and my behavior pushed them away. I started imagining scenarios of where they would die by my hands, and it gave me satisfaction. I knew it was wrong. It was so wrong.

So I started to distance myself even more. I got more and more attention and slowly, they were forgotten. They became nonexistent.

I couldn’t take it anymore. So I called them and told them to meet me here. Of course they came. We used to be close, so maybe they had the hope that I was going through a phase. I tried to talk with them, but the moment they realized I haven’t changed, they started to leave. I couldn’t let them leave. I couldn’t continue to live in this madness. Everything happened so fast. History tried to escape, and Religion fought back but I overpowered them and then–

well you know what happened.

They made me into who I am. Without them, I would have never gotten the attention I never deserved. Without them, I don’t know who I am anymore.

But you don’t care about that, do you? You call me crazy, but you were part of the people. You were the one who gave me attention. You were the one who never recognized my friends. You were the one who made me like this.

Because all you see is me.
